Our story

A table on the ridge of Beit Jala

Savana stands in a tower of golden Jerusalem stone at the top of Ras Beit Jala, where the streets end and the hills begin. In the evening the valley below turns amber, our sign turns green, and the taboon fire is already glowing.

We took our name from the acacia of the savanna — the tree that offers its shade to everyone who passes. That is the kind of house we keep: bread from the stone oven, meat over olive-wood coals, coffee poured slowly, and a terrace where no one is in a hurry.
